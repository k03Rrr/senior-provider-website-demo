How I Work

In professional settings, smooth communication is rarely accidental. It follows from preparation, experience across different environments, and practical decisions taken before discussions begin: understanding the context, identifying likely sensitivities, and clarifying what matters for the assignment.

The objective is simple: no unnecessary friction, no avoidable confusion, and no surprises that could have been addressed earlier.

01

Preparation

Before any assignment begins, the work has already started. Understanding the subject matter and core vocabulary, including professional jargon where relevant, is an important part of the process. This usually means reviewing briefing materials carefully, researching terminology in both directions, and speaking with the client and - if necessary - colleagues beforehand.

It is equally important to understand the context: who will be involved, what the meeting needs to achieve, where sensitivities may arise, and what practical details should be clarified in advance. In both technically complex environments and discreet private assignments, prior familiarity allows preparation to focus on what is specific to the conversation rather than starting from scratch.

Work may involve on-site events across the United Kingdom and the EU, remote participation, or hybrid formats requiring co-ordination across multiple locations, time zones, and, where relevant, several interpretation teams. A dedicated studio-grade interpretation set-up, including back-up arrangements, allows remote assignments to be handled with continuity and reliability.

Scheduling, travel, technical requirements, and co-ordination are handled directly and without unnecessary complexity. Questions are raised early and only where necessary, helping everyone remain focused on substance rather than process.

02

During an Assignment

Assignments take many forms. Faithful and complete rendering is non-negotiable throughout.

In legal and judicial settings, this includes a speaker's own hesitations, corrections, and precise formulations, as they may carry procedural or evidential weight. In conference, institutional, and private settings, the same commitment to accuracy is combined with judgements about register, pace, and emphasis that help ensure the message arrives as intended.

03

Discretion

All assignments are treated with strict confidentiality - as a matter of professional codes of conduct across all contexts, and as a matter of obligation in judicial, arbitration, and investigative settings. This is not a formality. It is a working condition.
